The 12-step output remained structurally close to the baseline. The 20-step
output changed the scene composition, not just texture or color. Since the
exact-processor calibration at 20 steps stayed much closer to baseline, the
long-run divergence is coming from the K3 key compression accumulating across
the denoising trajectory.

## Interpretation

The tracked module-49 policy is a short-horizon candidate, not a general SDXL
policy yet. This is exactly the diffusion-specific behavior we expected might
show up: a layer can look safe in captured tensors and short image runs, then
become unsafe as denoising steps accumulate error.

The next policy mechanism should include at least one of:

1. Timestep gating: enable K3 only on later, less fragile steps.
2. Higher K precision: test K4 or K5 for module 49 at 20 steps.
3. Residual scaling: reduce QJL correction strength if the sign residual is
   over-steering longer trajectories.
